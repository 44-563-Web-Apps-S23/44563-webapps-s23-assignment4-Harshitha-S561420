Harshitha snips a play

SAM AVERAGE _Percy Mackaye

_Near the centre, left, against the dark, a flag with stars floats from its standard._

_Beside the fire_, ANDREW, _reclined, gazes at a small frame in his hand; near him is a knapsack, with contents emptied beside it_.

_On the embankment_, JOEL, _with a gun, paces back and forth, a blanket thrown about his shoulders_.

JOEL

[_With a singing call._]

Four o'clock!--All's well!

[_Jumping down from the embankment, he approaches the fire._]

ANDREW
.

By God, Joel, it's bitter.

JOEL

[_Rubbing his hands over the coals._]

A mite sharpish.

ANDREW

[_Looks up eagerly._]

What?

JOEL
.

Cuts sharp, for Thanksgivin'.

ANDREW

[_Sinks back, gloomily._]

Oh!

[_A pause._]

I wondered you should agree with me. You meant the weather. I meant--

[_A pause again._]

JOEL
.

Well, Andy, what'd you mean?
